Introduction

The Renaissance was a period of advances in technology and science in various areas. Medicine, astronomy and geography were some of the areas which were advanced in the renaissance period. Within medicine they did experiments on the human body to extend their knowledge about it. In astronomy there were many great scientific advances, there were new theories about the solar system and there were discoveries about new stars and sunspots. The printing press was also invented in the Renaissance and it was invented by a German man named Johannes Gutenberg. The Renaissance was an era of great men such as Leonardo da Vinci, Galileo Galilei, Filippo Brunelleschi and many more.

Astronomy

Around the end of the Renaissance the man called Galileo Galilei who was a scientist in his age invented a telescope. When Galilei found out that there was an object which could enhance you view length by many times, he was so intrigued that he studied it all night. He built one himself which had three times the zoom of a normal eye, and as time passed he enhanced it to 33 times magnification. With this newly created “binoculars” he looked up in the sky which led him to extraordinary discoveries. Galilei discovered that there was an uncountable number of stars, the craters and mountains on the moon, the planet Venus different phases, what looked like Saturn’s rings and many more discoveries. Galilei published “Sidereus Nuncius” which was a short astronomical book that featured his early discoveries using the telescope which he made.
