Introduction

The Renaissance was a period of advances in technology and science in various areas. Medicine, astronomy and geography were some of the areas which were advanced in the renaissance period. Within medicine they did experiments on the human body to extend their knowledge about it. In astronomy there were many great scientific advances, there were new theories about the solar system and there were discoveries about new stars and sunspots. The printing press was also invented in the Renaissance and it was invented by a German man named Johannes Gutenberg. The Renaissance was an era of great men such as Leonardo da Vinci, Galileo Galilei, Filippo Brunelleschi and many more.

Astronomy

Around the end of the Renaissance the man called Galileo Galilei who was a scientist in his age invented a telescope. When Galilei found out that there was an object which could enhance you view length by many times, he was so intrigued that he studied it all night. He built one himself which had three times the zoom of a normal eye, and as time passed he enhanced it to 33 times magnification. With this newly created “binoculars” he looked up in the sky which led him to extraordinary discoveries. Galilei discovered that there was an uncountable number of stars, the craters and mountains on the moon, the planet Venus different phases, what looked like Saturn’s rings and many more discoveries. Galilei published “Sidereus Nuncius” which was a short astronomical book that featured his early discoveries using the telescope which he made. This is a picture from “Sidereus Nuncius” that shows the moons different phases.

The many ideas about how the planets and stars were aligned were changed during the Renaissance by different people. It was believed that the earth was in the middle of the Milky Way and not the sun, this was because of the Catholic Church which deemed a new model of the universe would border upon blasphemy. The idea that the sun lays in the centre of our universe came from Copernicus which later in the Renaissance was built on by Johannes Kepler and Galileo Galilei in various ways. Johannes Kepler believed that the universe was geometrically perfect and that it could be described mathematically which would be much better instead of using epicycles within epicycles on a model.

Medicine

During the Renaissance experiments on the human body was done, they dissected the human body to extend their knowledge about human anatomy. One of the greatest contributors to knowledge about human anatomy is Leonardo Da Vinci, who also was an artist that was skilled at drawing the human body. In the Hospital of Santa Maria Nuova in Florence Leonardo was given permission to dissect human corpses which led him to write 13 000 words and paint more than 240 pictures about the human anatomy during the year he was at the hospital. Leonardo's drawings consist of the human skeleton and its parts, muscles, sinews, the heart, the sex organs and other parts of the anatomy. If Leonardo Da Vinci’s drawings would have been published they would have been a great contribution to medical science, all was not published but some were. When Leonardo Da Vinci died his heir took over the work of compiling all of his work to a treatise but only a small amount of what Leonardo had written down was included in his treatise on painting because of Leonardo's unique writing style.
