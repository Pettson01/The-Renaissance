This war had the direct consequence of shaping the religious map of central Europe for hundreds
of years to come. <br><br> This is also the war that marked the beginning of the Swedish Empire.
This war is one of the longest and most brutal wars in European history, claiming 8 million lives
and lasting for as the name suggests 30 years.<br><br>
The main causes of this war was the new Emperor of the Holy Roman Empire, Ferdinand II. One
of his first actions as Emperor was to force his citizens to adhere to the Roman Catholicism,
directly defying the peace treaty of Augsburg, which stated the citizens of the Holy Roman
Empire had religious freedom.<br><br>
The Swedish Empire led by Gustav II Adolph saw many successes in this war. They were able to
secure large parts of northern Germany and this war marked the beginning of the Swedish
Empire.
